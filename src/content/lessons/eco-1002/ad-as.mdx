## AD: the price-level slice of IS-LM

Holding the rest of the IS-LM apparatus fixed, vary $P$. Higher $P$ shrinks
real balances $M/P$, shifting LM left, raising $r$, and lowering $Y$. The
resulting $(Y, P)$ schedule is **aggregate demand**.

## SRAS: prices catch up to expectations

In the short run firms set prices based on expected prices $P^e$ and
unit-cost pressures. A common parameterization is

$$
P = P^e + c\,(Y - Y_n),
$$

where $Y_n$ is the natural rate of output. SRAS slopes upward in $(Y, P)$.

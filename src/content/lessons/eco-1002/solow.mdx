## Setup

Output per worker is Cobb-Douglas: $y = k^{\alpha}$. Capital evolves with
investment $s\,y$, population growth $n$, and depreciation $\delta$:

$$
\dot k \;=\; s\,k^{\alpha} - (n + \delta)\,k.
$$

## Steady state

Setting $\dot k = 0$:

$$
k^\* = \left(\frac{s}{n + \delta}\right)^{\!1/(1 - \alpha)}, \qquad
y^\* = (k^\*)^{\alpha}.
$$

The growth-accounting punch line: in steady state, output per worker is
constant in $k$ — long-run growth must come from technology, not saving more.

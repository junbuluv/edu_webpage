## Overview

Why firms hedge: tax convexity, distress avoidance, managerial risk aversion. Tools: forwards, futures, swaps, options. The cost-benefit of hedging.

## Why it matters

This lesson maps to Berk & DeMarzo *Corporate Finance* 6e, Chapter 30. The decisions covered here recur in nearly every later unit: every valuation, capital-structure, and capital-budgeting question reduces to applying one or more of the ideas introduced here.

## What you'll be able to do

After completing this lesson and its practice problems, you should be able to execute each learning objective above on a textbook problem set or a real-world short case. Worked examples follow the Berk-DeMarzo notation conventions; the interactive components on the page let you stress-test the model with sliders.

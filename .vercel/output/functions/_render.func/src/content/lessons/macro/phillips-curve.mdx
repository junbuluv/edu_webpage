## The expectations-augmented form

$$
\pi_t \;=\; \pi^e_t - \beta\,(u_t - u_n) + \varepsilon_t.
$$

Inflation rises above expected inflation when unemployment is below its
natural rate. With adaptive or rational expectations updating to $\pi^e_t$,
sustained low unemployment shifts the entire curve up.

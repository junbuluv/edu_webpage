Aggregate Demand and Short-Run Supply

Output and the price level as the joint outcome of AD and SRAS clearing.

⏱ 20 min Tags: AD-AS, supply shocks, demand shocks

Learning objectives

  • Derive AD from IS-LM with a price level.
  • Explain the slope of SRAS under sticky expectations.
  • Predict the short-run effects of demand and supply shocks on (Y, P).

AD: the price-level slice of IS-LM

Holding the rest of the IS-LM apparatus fixed, vary PPP. Higher PPP shrinks real balances M/PM/PM/P, shifting LM left, raising rrr, and lowering YYY. The resulting (Y,P)(Y, P)(Y,P) schedule is aggregate demand.

SRAS: prices catch up to expectations

In the short run firms set prices based on expected prices PeP^ePe and unit-cost pressures. A common parameterization is

P=Pe+c (Y−Yn),P = P^e + c\,(Y - Y_n),P=Pe+c(Y−Yn​),

where YnY_nYn​ is the natural rate of output. SRAS slopes upward in (Y,P)(Y, P)(Y,P).

Equilibrium

Y* = 1030, P* = 4.00

Try a fiscal expansion (↑ G), a monetary expansion (↑ M), and a stagflationary supply shock (↑ PeP^ePe). Notice that the two demand shocks move YYY and PPP in the same direction; the supply shock moves them in opposite directions.

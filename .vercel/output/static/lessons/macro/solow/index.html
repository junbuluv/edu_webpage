The Solow Growth Model

Capital accumulation, diminishing returns, and the steady state under Cobb-Douglas production.

⏱ 20 min Tags: growth, Solow, Cobb-Douglas

Learning objectives

  • Write the Solow law of motion for k.
  • Solve for the steady-state capital and output per worker.
  • Predict how s, n, δ, and α move the steady state.

Setup

Output per worker is Cobb-Douglas: y=kαy = k^{\alpha}y=kα. Capital evolves with investment s ys\,ysy, population growth nnn, and depreciation δ\deltaδ:

k˙  =  s kα−(n+δ) k.\dot k \;=\; s\,k^{\alpha} - (n + \delta)\,k.k˙=skα−(n+δ)k.

Steady state

Setting k˙=0\dot k = 0k˙=0:

k\*=(sn+δ) ⁣1/(1−α),y\*=(k\*)α.k^\* = \left(\frac{s}{n + \delta}\right)^{\!1/(1 - \alpha)}, \qquad y^\* = (k^\*)^{\alpha}.k\*=(n+δs​)1/(1−α),y\*=(k\*)α.

The growth-accounting punch line: in steady state, output per worker is constant in kkk — long-run growth must come from technology, not saving more.

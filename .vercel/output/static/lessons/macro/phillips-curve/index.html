The Phillips Curve

Inflation as a function of expected inflation and the unemployment gap, with an interactive expectations-augmented version.

⏱ 15 min Tags: Phillips curve, expectations, natural rate

Learning objectives

  • State the expectations-augmented Phillips curve.
  • Distinguish movement along the curve from shifts in expectations.
  • Read off short-run vs long-run policy trade-offs.

The expectations-augmented form

πt  =  πte−β (ut−un)+εt.\pi_t \;=\; \pi^e_t - \beta\,(u_t - u_n) + \varepsilon_t.πt​=πte​−β(ut​−un​)+εt​.

Inflation rises above expected inflation when unemployment is below its natural rate. With adaptive or rational expectations updating to πte\pi^e_tπte​, sustained low unemployment shifts the entire curve up.

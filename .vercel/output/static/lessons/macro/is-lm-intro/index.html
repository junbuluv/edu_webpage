The IS-LM Model

Equilibrium output and the interest rate in a closed economy, derived from goods-market and money-market clearing.

⏱ 25 min Tags: IS-LM, fiscal policy, monetary policy

Learning objectives

  • Derive the IS curve from goods-market equilibrium.
  • Derive the LM curve from money-market equilibrium.
  • Predict how Y* and r* respond to fiscal and monetary shocks.

Goods market: the IS curve

The IS curve traces combinations of output YYY and the real interest rate rrr that clear the goods market. Starting from the national-income identity in a closed economy,

Y=C(Y−T)+I(r)+G,Y = C(Y - T) + I(r) + G,Y=C(Y−T)+I(r)+G,

and assuming a linear consumption function C=C0+c(Y−T)C = C_0 + c(Y - T)C=C0​+c(Y−T) with a tax rate T=tYT = tYT=tY and investment I=I0−brI = I_0 - b rI=I0​−br, solving for YYY gives the IS relation

YIS(r)  =  11−c(1−t)(C0+I0+G−b r).Y_{IS}(r) \;=\; \frac{1}{1 - c(1-t)} \big(C_0 + I_0 + G - b\,r\big).YIS​(r)=1−c(1−t)1​(C0​+I0​+G−br).

The slope is negative: a lower interest rate raises investment and, through the multiplier α=1/(1−c(1−t))\alpha = 1/(1 - c(1-t))α=1/(1−c(1−t)), raises equilibrium output.

Money market: the LM curve

Money-market equilibrium sets real money demand equal to real money supply:

MP=kY−hr⟹rLM(Y)=kY−M/Ph.\frac{M}{P} = k Y - h r \quad\Longrightarrow\quad r_{LM}(Y) = \frac{k Y - M/P}{h}.PM​=kY−hr⟹rLM​(Y)=hkY−M/P​.

LM is upward-sloping in (Y,r)(Y, r)(Y,r): higher output raises transactions demand for money, requiring a higher interest rate to clear the market at fixed M/PM/PM/P.

Setting YIS(r)=YY_{IS}(r) = YYIS​(r)=Y and rLM(Y)=rr_{LM}(Y) = rrLM​(Y)=r and solving simultaneously:

Y\*=αhA+b(M/P)h+αbk,r\*=kY\*−M/Ph,Y^\* = \frac{\alpha h A + b(M/P)}{h + \alpha b k}, \qquad r^\* = \frac{k Y^\* - M/P}{h},Y\*=h+αbkαhA+b(M/P)​,r\*=hkY\*−M/P​,

where A=C0+I0+GA = C_0 + I_0 + GA=C0​+I0​+G.
